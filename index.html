About Me

I recently completed my M.S. in Physics (GPA: 3.48) and operate as a Computational Quantum Theorist. My research philosophy is strictly "math-first": I rigorously derive analytical frameworks, such as optimal POVMs and generalized state discrimination, before validating them computationally on near-term quantum hardware. As a published first-author in AVS Quantum Science (Q1) and an active IBM Qiskit Advocate, my workflow bridges foundational theoretical physics with modern quantum programming (Python, Qiskit, PennyLane).

My current research focuses on simulating complex physical systems, ranging from entanglement dynamics to holographic toy models of black holes. Beyond research, I am deeply passionate about quantum pedagogy and am an active core member of QBangladesh working towards a sustainable quantum education environement in Bangladesh. My ultimate goal is to build an inclusive research environment in Bangladesh that challenges outdated curricula and empowers unconventional thinkers to tackle fundamental questions of nature.
